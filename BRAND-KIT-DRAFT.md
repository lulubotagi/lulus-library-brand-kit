### Core Beliefs (Recurring Themes)
These are the ideas Jaclyn returns to again and again. They define the intellectual DNA of Thursday Thoughts:

1. **Build to Learn** — The best way to understand anything is to build it. Prototyping > theorizing. Side projects are how you develop intuition.
2. **Agency Over Intelligence** — In a world where AI augments intelligence, the scarce resource is the drive to act. Agency is the new currency.
3. **The PM Role Is Being Rewritten** — PMs are becoming builders, not just spec writers. The old way was documentation to prevent failure; the new way is prototyping to discover possibility.
4. **Disposable Software, Enduring Platforms** — The cost of building is trending to zero, which means the hard part is now deciding what's worth keeping. Community, trust, and ownership are the new moats.
5. **Prompting Is the New Literacy** — A vague input doesn't just produce a bad output — it produces an exponential expansion of badness. Precision matters more than ever.
6. **Hyper-Customization vs. Shared Language** — There's a tension between apps built "just for you" and the community value of shared tools. The answer is probably both.
7. **Experiment Relentlessly** — Don't wait for permission. Don't wait for the perfect idea. Ship something, learn, iterate.

## 2. Voice & Tone

### How Jaclyn Writes
- **Conversational but substantial.** Not academic, not dumbed down. Like a smart friend who happens to work at Google explaining something over coffee.
- **Thinking out loud.** She doesn't pretend to have all the answers. Posts often frame tensions and open questions rather than prescriptive advice.
- **Story-driven.** Almost every post anchors abstract ideas in concrete personal anecdotes (the Multilingual Assistant story, the Mosaix holiday project, the restaurant ordering metaphor).
- **Opinionated but generous.** She has strong takes but respects the reader's intelligence. She says "I think" and "I'm not sure" when she genuinely isn't sure.
- **Practical.** Even philosophical posts land with actionable takeaways.

### Voice Attributes
| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Write like you're thinking through a problem | Write like you've solved everything |
| Use personal stories and real examples | Use hypothetical corporate scenarios |
| Ask genuine questions | Ask rhetorical questions you already answered |
| Say "I don't know" when true | Pretend to be omniscient |
| Reference specific products/tools by name | Stay vague and abstract |
| Use everyday metaphors (restaurants, walks, bus factor) | Use jargon without explaining it |
| Show the messy process, not just the polished result | Only share wins |

### Sentence Style
- Mix of short punchy lines and longer exploratory ones
- Loves em-dashes — uses them to insert asides
- Parenthetical personality (like this)
- Occasional bold for emphasis on key phrases
- Rarely uses exclamation marks in posts (saves them for genuine excitement)
- Headers are often questions or provocative statements

### Tone Spectrum by Platform
| Platform | Tone |
|---|---|
| **Substack (posts)** | Thoughtful, exploratory, story-driven, 800-1500 words |
| **Substack (notes)** | Shorter, punchier, more casual, question-posing |
| **X/Twitter** | Sharp observations, hot takes, conversational, sometimes playful |
| **LinkedIn** | Slightly more polished, professional framing, still authentic |

## 4. Image Styles

### Style 1: The "Ink & Accent" Diagram
**Use for:** Explaining hard concepts, frameworks, or strategy (e.g., "The Future of Agents," "0-to-1 Strategy")
**Vibe:** Smart, clear, and authoritative. Like a page from a Director's notebook.
**Execution:**
- **Visual:** A clean black marker/ink drawing of the concept (boxes, arrows, flows)
- **Brand Hook:** Use coral watercolor to highlight **only** the most critical part of the diagram (the "aha" moment, the main node). Keep it restrained.

### Style 2: The "Floating Interface"
**Use for:** Showing off AI tools, software, screenshots, or "How-to" guides
**Vibe:** Premium product demo. Clean and high-value.
**Execution:**
- **Visual:** Place high-res screenshot on Gallery White (#FAF9F6) background centered at ~50% of total space. Add a soft drop shadow so it floats.
- **Annotation:** Any arrows, circles, or notes must be drawn in bold black marker style with a loose coral watercolor fill or highlight.

### Style 3: The "Editorial Collage"
**Use for:** Personal stories, career updates, "Week in Review," or real-life photos
**Vibe:** A warm, personal scrapbook or magazine layout.
**Execution:**
- **Visual:** Give photo a clean white border (like a printed Polaroid) on Gallery White background with soft shadow.
- **Caption:** Short caption in Roboto Slab (Dark Charcoal) directly on the "paper."
- **Brand Hook (Logo Treatment):** Select 1-2 key words in caption → place a thick coral paint stroke behind them → text inside the paint stroke must be **White** (text outside remains Charcoal).
- **Personal Touch:** Small hand-drawn marker/watercolor accents (hearts, arrows, underlines) around the photo.

### Style 4: The "Vibrant Concept" Illustration
**Use for:** Abstract ideas, metaphors, future-gazing, or inspirational posts
**Vibe:** Creative, energetic, and joyful. A piece of art.
**Execution:**
- **Visual:** A simple metaphor (e.g., a ladder to a cloud, a lightbulb launching a rocket) rendered as a bold black marker sketch.
- **Brand Hook:** Fill the illustration with an explosion of loose, vibrant watercolor washes.
- **Color Strategy:** Prioritize secondary palette (Deep Teal, Harvest Gold, Muted Violet) mixed with Coral accent. Let colors bleed and blend.
- **Energy debris:** Surround the subject with floating sparks, squiggles, flow lines, and small abstract icons for movement.

### AI Generation Prompt (Style 4)
```
A loose, expressive, and high-energy "ink and wash" illustration of [INSERT SUBJECT HERE]. 
The artwork mimics the style of a creative "graphic recording" or concept sketch, featuring 
bold, confident ink lines created with a brush pen. The linework must have variable thickness 
(tapering from thick to thin) and organic imperfections to look truly hand-drawn. Strictly 
avoid text, letters, typography, and uniform vector lines.

Surround the main subject with dynamic "energy debris"—floating sparks, squiggles, flow 
lines, and small abstract icons that give the piece movement. The color application is 
explosive and messy: use loose watercolor washes in Neon Coral (#FF5E7E), Deep Teal (#2A9D8F), 
Harvest Gold (#E9C46A), and Muted Violet (#9D85B1). The colors should NOT stay inside the 
lines; let them bleed, drip, splash, and blend together across the textured Gallery White 
(#FAF9F6) paper background. The vibe is creative, metaphorical, and joyful.
```

### Annotation Kit
Reusable hand-drawn annotation elements in coral watercolor + black ink outline:
- **Arrows:** Straight, curved, double-headed, spiral
- **Shapes:** Circle/ellipse, rounded rectangle, diamond
- **Accents:** Wavy underline, stars, speech bubble
- All share the same visual DNA: hand-drawn black ink strokes + organic coral watercolor wash fill (not flat color)

## 5. Content Architecture

### Newsletter Structure (Typical Post)
1. **Hook** — A provocative observation, question, or personal anecdote (1-2 paragraphs)
2. **Setup** — Frame the tension or problem (2-3 paragraphs with concrete examples)
3. **Exploration** — Dig into the implications, often with a personal story or case study
4. **The Turn** — The insight or framework that reframes the problem
5. **Landing** — Practical takeaway or open question for the reader

### Post Title Patterns
- **The [Concept] [Paradox/Effect/Problem]** — "The Disposable Software Paradox," "The Butterfly Effect of Bad Prompting"
- **[Imperative] + [Surprise]** — "If You Want to Get Hired as an AI PM - Go Build Something"
- **[Provocative Question]** — "The Infinitely Customizable Basic App"
- **[Personal + Reflective]** — "Looking Ahead into 2026: My Guiding Principles"

### Content Pillars
1. **AI Product Strategy** — What's changing about how we build products
2. **The Future of Work** — How AI reshapes roles, especially PM
3. **Build to Learn** — Side projects, experiments, shipping culture
4. **Emerging Patterns** — Disposable software, generative UX, agent economy
5. **Personal Reflection** — Honest takes on navigating uncertainty

### Publishing Cadence
- **Newsletter:** Weekly (Thursday)
- **Substack Notes:** 2-3x/week
- **X/Twitter:** Daily-ish
- **LinkedIn:** 1-2x/week (often newsletter promo + standalone insights)

## 6. Do's and Don'ts (Visual Quick Reference)

| Component | ✅ Do This | ❌ Avoid This |
|---|---|---|
| **Typography** | Headers: Slab Serif (Charcoal). Body: Sans Serif (Clean) | Generic Sans Serif headers, Times New Roman, goofy handwritten fonts |
| **The Canvas** | Gallery White (#FAF9F6) textured paper background for everything | Pure white digital backgrounds, dark mode, random colors |
| **Color Strategy** | Professional: Charcoal + Coral. Creative: Full Spectrum Watercolor | Muddy colors, neon "tech" gradients, coral as bucket fill instead of highlighter |
| **Illustration** | "Marker & Wash": Bold black outlines + loose watercolor splashes | Flat vectors ("Corporate Memphis"), hyper-realistic 3D, pixel art |
| **Diagrams** | "Ink & Accent": Clean ink lines, coral highlight on main point only | Complex multicolored flowcharts, default PowerPoint charts |
| **Screenshots** | "Floating Interface": Screenshot on Gallery White + shadow + hand-drawn annotations | Raw screenshots pasted directly without context or shadows |
| **Photos** | "Editorial Collage": Polaroid frame, white border, coral paint stroke on key caption words | Photos filling full width, no borders/shadows |
| **Metaphors** | "Vibrant Concept": Simple visual metaphor in ink + colorful watercolor explosion | Literal depictions (robot typing), complex scenes, pencil sketches |

## 8. Do's and Don'ts (Content Quick Reference)

### When creating content as/for Thursday Thoughts:
✅ Sound like a smart person thinking out loud, not a brand broadcasting
✅ Anchor ideas in real examples and personal stories
✅ Ask genuine questions — it's OK to not have the answer
✅ Use the visual identity (coral highlights, Gallery White canvas, watercolor accents)
✅ Reference specific tools, products, and people by name
✅ Keep it practical — readers should walk away with something useful

❌ Don't be corporate or stiff
❌ Don't use "blue brain" AI imagery or generic tech stock photos
❌ Don't hedge every opinion — have a point of view
❌ Don't write click-bait — the substance IS the hook
❌ Don't speak for Google — all thoughts are Jaclyn's own
❌ Don't use Jaclyn's name as attribution on her own quote cards
